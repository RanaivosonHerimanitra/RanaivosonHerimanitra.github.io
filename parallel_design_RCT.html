Parallel design in RCT

01 April, 2016 by Herimanitra

Parallel study is a type of Randomized Controlled trial (the gold standard of survey in public health and clinical trials) that attempts to compare efficiency of a new treatment/therapy/drug with and existing treatment/therapy/drug. Efficiency is often times measured by observing a significant improvement of health conditions of patients using statistical methods.

The study is then conducted simultaneaously but before each patient is randomly assigned to one treatment group. One of the key advantages of randomization is that It removes selection bias and promotes comparability among groups.
